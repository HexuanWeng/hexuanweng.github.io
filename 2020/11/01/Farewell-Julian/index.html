Farewell, Julian

Posted on 2020-11-01 Edited on 2021-08-28 In Simple Life
I feel so lucky to meet Julian's family.

Connie is my savior. I knew Connie in 2019 when I worked in Stomble with Garlio, who is Connie’s younger brother. He recommended me to his sister’s language center to teach English-speakers mandarin. Cathy interviewed me and I joined CCLingo language center successfully in June, 2019. Connie is the founder of CCL. At that moment, she was an employee at Microsoft. In retrospect, Connie is the answer to my prayer: she provided me a wonderful platform at CCLingo and this April she introduced me to Happy Angels Home to work as a disability support worker. I spent 6 months in total with Julian, who is an autism teenager living in Penshurst.

Julian graduated from a special school in 2018, and then he started studying in a day program in Caringbah. It is a program designed for the disabled to study together after graduation. However, due to the pandamic, Julian’s previous day program suspended and he had to stay at home until the epidemic is over. That’s why I got this opportunity to work with Julian and spent such wonderful time with him.

I still remember the first day I met Julian, he was quite ingoing, listening to the conversation between Katherine (Julian’s mom) and I. This was my first time contacting with children with autism. Julian is non-verbal and he also has some uncontrollable behaviour on a daily basis. I was kind of nervous on the first day, because I feel it was quiet exhausted to explain everything to him and I had no idea whether he can understand what I said or not. I supposed to teach Julian 3 to 4 hours at the beginning, but on the first day, I finished my teaching within 2 hours with a sense of frustration.

It seems unbelievable that I feel enjoyable to teach Julian later on when I found the way to teach him. We did Reading Eggs together, which is a kind of Australian textbook for children from year 3 to year 13. I was astonished to see Julian’s progress day by day. He is amazing and I gained a sense of accomplishment when I tutored him gradually. I also teach him mathematics. To my surprised, he loves maths more than other subjects. Different from us, he is good at mental arithmetic. He has obsessive-compulsive disorder in study, for example, if he haven’t finished his homework, he won’t start doing the next tasks. I enjoyed the time staying with him. He showed me the potential he can be smarter. When I first talked with Julian, he can’t answer my questions but repeat my questions on the iPad. As I have mentioned, he can’t speak because he is a non-verbal. The only way to communicate is to use a iPad. He was accustomed to repeating questions over and over again rather than understand the question and answer it. It is quiet hard to practice this communication skills at the beginning, because he used to talk like a parrot and just repeat what he heard. However, since September, Julian can answer some simple questions using his communication tool without repeating questions like a parrot.

Julian can play piano!! And he doesn’t need to look at the piano textbook while playing the song. I love Princess Waltz he played. I was surprised that he has such a wonderful gift, because I don’t know any tunes but he can read music. We also do some exercises at the yard in front of his house. He was a slim boy when he was young, but now he seems getting stronger and fatter than before. 😂 Julian’s favorite thing to do is to take the train! He loves to sit on smoothly moving objects like trains. I also noticed that he watched vlog related to train and played computer games related to train. Once I asked him, “Do you want to become a train driver?” He nodded. I took him to Miranda shopping center and he can remember the way to the train station. When he saw the train drive slowly in, he seemed excited.

We drew pictures every Saturday, went shopping every Friday and really had a good time together! However, I have to say googbe to Julian’s family temporarily, because I will start my journey at ASI solution as a developer tomorrow. No one knows the way forward, but just work hard!

Goodbye, Julian~
I will miss you~
